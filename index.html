01 / About

Background

Four years at PwC Mumbai taught me what companies look like from the inside. I started in audit, qualified as a CA on the first attempt, then spent three years rebuilding finance functions. One project freed ₹20 Cr of working capital for a retailer, another took a 7-day financial close down to 1 day.

But consulting shows you a company after the decisions are made. The judgment I want to learn happens earlier: should this deal be done at all, and at what price. So I started doing that work in public: a venture memo on Dust, a €10.7bn Leveraged Buyout (LBO) rebuilt from scratch, and a Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) model where my own math talked me out of my initial thesis. In September I start the Bocconi × ESSEC double degree (one of 25 selected); outside work, four years with PwC Foundation mentoring 45+ women on employability, and now learning Italian and German for the move to Milan.

Most stories about a business are true and not the point. I care about the two or three numbers that actually decide whether a deal works, and what has to stay true for them to hold. Red-teaming my own work isn't a formality at the end, it's the only part of the process that tells me if I'm right for the right reasons.
