01 / ABOUT

A numbers person who ended up liking words too.

I qualified as a Chartered Accountant on my first attempt, then spent four years at PwC inside Finance Transformation and GCC Advisory — the part of consulting where you're rebuilding how a company actually reports and reconciles, not just advising on it from a slide. Along the way I helped unlock ₹20 Cr in working capital for a retail client and shaved a seven-day finance close down to one.

Somewhere in that process I got pulled toward the earlier, messier end of finance — venture capital and private equity, where the numbers are still being invented rather than reconciled. That pull is why I built VC-Lab, why I write about markets on the side, and why I'm now one of 25 students in the Bocconi × ESSEC Double Degree, using it to build a bridge between Indian markets and European ones.

Outside of spreadsheets: I've run workshops and mentored early-career women through PwC's community programs, I'm slowly getting functional in Italian and German ahead of the move to Milan, and I follow Indian equities the way other people follow sport.
